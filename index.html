About Me

My name is Katherine Smith. I was born and raised in Augusta, Georgia : a town known for the Masters Golf Tournament and not much else. It was always a town people descibed as "a town that everyone complains about, but no one ever leaves." It will always be home to me. Growing up, I a very active kid and it was a rare occasion if I was not outside. I played soccer since I could walk, was a competitive volleyball player in high school. In addition to sports, I also had another passion: school. I always excelled in school and genuinely enjoyed learning and growing. My favorite subject was always math, with science as a close second. Because of this, my college major will come as no surprise.

I graduated high school in May of 2014, and was very eager to start my college career at The University of Georgia. I always knew I had an interest in the medical field; however, I was a little weary of dealing with the emotional side of working in a clinical setting as a doctor. This is why I decided against a pre-med track and chose to major in biomedical engineering. In my 3rd year at the University, I decided to add on a certificate in computer science to give myself another skill and to open more doors in my career path journey.

As one could probably imagine, my college journey has been one with many struggles, many tears, and an unimaginable amount of stress. It also has given me a lot of smiles, life-long friendships, and unforgetable experiences. Most importantly, my time at UGA has given me a newfound sense of confidence that I am capable and competent to do what I set my mind to. Although my journey is not yet finished, I cannot wait to see what my last year at UGA will bring me and what my future will hold.
